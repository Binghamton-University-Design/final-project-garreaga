The Permian Basin Superorganism, Immanis Colosseus, also known as Mystery Flesh Pit, is a subterranean organism unique to modern biology. Being the sole occupant of the Phylum Immanemqa she is quite special.

After her discovery by a pilot well drilling crew in 1973, more and more efforts were made to find out how big she really is, but we don’t know yet :(. She can span up to hundreds of miles beneath the surface, potentially into the upper mantle of Earth’s interior~

Tissue samples suggest that the organism contains gastrointestinal, vascular, respiratory, musculoskeletal, nervous, limbic, and integumentary systems super similar to mammal life... She’s several hundred thousand years old, too.
